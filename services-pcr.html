Common Applications

  • Impurity Detection and Quantification: Employing dPCR to detect nucleic acid impurities, contaminants, including residual host cell DNA or RNA, ensuring that biologic substances are of the highest purity and meet regulatory standards.
  • Viral Load Determination: Accurately measuring viral load in biologics like vaccines to ensure the product's safety and efficacy.
  • Gene Therapy Product Characterization: Utilizing dPCR to analyze and quantify specific gene sequences, ensuring the correct genetic material's presence, quantity, and quality.
  • Monitoring Genetic Modifications: Detecting and quantifying genetic changes in biologics, confirming the successful introduction of desired modifications.
  • Nucleic Acid-Based Therapeutics Validation: Validating the presence and quantity of therapeutic nucleic acids in biologic products.
  • Batch-to-Batch Consistency: Ensuring consistent product quality by comparing genetic or nucleic acid profiles across different batches.
  • Detection of Rare Mutations: Tailoring treatments by detecting and quantifying specific genetic mutations, especially beneficial in personalized medicine approaches.
  • Quality Control and Release Testing: Serving as a rigorous quality control method, dPCR validates the purity, integrity, and authenticity of biologic products before market release.
